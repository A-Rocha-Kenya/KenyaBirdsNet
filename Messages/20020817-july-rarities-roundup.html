Subject: July rarities roundup!
Dear all,

Over the past week, several very interesting observations came to our 
notice. Even though the birds were seen several weeks ago, that doesn't mean 
they're not there anymore....

On 6th July, a potential first for Kenya was observed and photographed by a 
visiting Dutch birder, Jan Bisschop: a summer adult Arctic Tern, at Sabaki 
River mouth. Descriptions and pictures will be submitted to the Rarities 
Committee.

In early July, a Dunlin in summer plumage was found at the northern en of 
Lake Nakuru by Steve Easly.

In late July, Nigel Redman and group found a Dickinson's Kestrel along the 
road to the Thika settling ponds.

Good birding!
Bernd
