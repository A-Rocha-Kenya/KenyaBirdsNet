Subject: farmland birding near Cherangani

Greetings all,

Over the Christmas holidays I did some birding in the farms around Kapcherop near Cherangani at about 2000 m while staying at the family farm.  It rained every day while we were there (Dec 23-27th).  Here’s the list:

Immature black-chested snake eagle (first time seeing this species here)

Pair of Harrier hawks

Brown parrots (many)  - resident at the farm

Crowned hornbill – nesting in an Acacia tree near the house

Speckled pigeons

Ring-necked dove

Red-eyed dove

Speckled mousebird

Barn swallows – 20-30 were regularly perched on the powerlines

Eurasian bee-eaters – 10 or so were near the barn swallows on the powerlines

Lilac-breasted roller – first time seen at the farm

African pied wagtail

Bulbul

Blue-eared starlings

White-eyed slaty flycatcher

African blue flycatcher – resident, seen every year

Southern(?) black flycatcher

Brown babblers – seem to be regular visitors over the past two years

African thrush

Tawny-flanked prinia

Yellow white-eye

Bronze sunbird

Scarlet-chested sunbird

Baglafecht’s weaver

Red-cheeked cordon bleu

Red-billed firefinch

Bronze manikin

Red-rumped swallow

African crowned eagle – immature, first time seen at the farm

African green pigeons – regular visitors at the fig tree

Black& white casqued hornbill – first time seeing this species at the farm

 

Happy new year,

Darcy Ogada
