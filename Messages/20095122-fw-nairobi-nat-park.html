Dear All,
 
Just to report that on sunny afternoon on 16th July in the Park one Whinchat along the watercourse from Hyena dam.   Also two pairs Secretary birds, male and female Saddle Bills and pair of crowned crane with one youngster.  Large flocks of common Quelea at Karen Primary School Dam but no sign of the yellow crowned bishops.   Large flocks of white winged widow birds but no sign of Jacksons throughout the area.
Yesterday,20th,   on a brief overcast afternoon visit to the Park, no sign of the winchat but we saw a Madagascar Squacco on the dam near Kisembe Forest, which we have seen before in that area.    The white winged Widow birds were again present but in smaller numbers.
One splendid Martial eagle by the Nag. Dam.
 
On 16th July we were very sad to see that the Epaulette bats have been BANISHED from the KWS main gate park entrance roof.  (A tracery of 'bat proof' wires have been strung across the entire under-roof canopy).   We wonder whether this was really necessary as they were of great interest and quite unusual.   
 
Karen Plumbe and Heather Elkins
