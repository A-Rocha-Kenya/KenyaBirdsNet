Thanks Colin,
It would be interesting to learn if there are quite a number of
WFscops in the cashew plantations, I don't think there is too much
else, but you never know if you don't look.
Glad you saw the Violet-breasted Sunbirds, I estimated there were 100
birds at least moving between the flowering Combretums. As we know so
little about this species, because there was a constant flow of birds
arriving and departing at bush height, I thought that if this happens
again next mid-April it would be interesting to ring them. It would
help to estimate numbers as well as hopefully lead to controls in the
future. I don't think you would get a recovery but you never know. Can
you send me a photo of the bird you caught at Mwamba. I have never
ever seen a good photograph of the species, although I did get some
nice video of them back in April. Amazingly on my July tour there was
a male at the lily pond near the Sabaki Bridge.
Very best for now
Brian
