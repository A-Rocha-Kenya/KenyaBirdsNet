As the house crow is one of Kenya's most serious vertebrate invasive species, this problem should be of great concern as well to the IUCN/SSC Invasive Species Specialist Group [  http://www.issg.org/ ]]]  , to IUCN, to BirdLife, and to many other conservation bodies...all of which should get involved in ameliorating and eliminating this threat.   
 
Are BirdLife and the IUCN Eastern Regional Office in Langata aware of this problem?  IUCN has the capacity to reach the highest levels in the Kenya Government....as, of course, do NEMA and KWS.
 
Reducing, then eliminating, the great negative impact that house crows have on Kenya's biodiversity would go a long way, in the eyes of the international conservation community, towards Kenya meeting its long-term biodiversity conservation commitments.
 
Tom
