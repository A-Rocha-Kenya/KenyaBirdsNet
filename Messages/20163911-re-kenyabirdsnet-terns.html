Gosh!, What a lot of words sparked by my mail of 5 December!

When I wrote in I was simply giving the English name that had been applied to this bird since at least 2008 in four lists — one of which, the IOC World List — purports to be the standard world list of English names.

In no way was I condoning the name!

There is, however, a serious point behind this, and that is the wisdom (or necessity) of having lists of “official” English names. I leave that to others to debate.

Best wishes,

Graeme

What I wrote:
". . . but note that this bird has been called just White-winged Tern for quite a while and is so called in the 4th ed of the Kenya Checklist (2008), the IOC World List v. 6.4 (2016), Howard & Moore 4 (2013) and by del Hoyo & Collar (2014).”
