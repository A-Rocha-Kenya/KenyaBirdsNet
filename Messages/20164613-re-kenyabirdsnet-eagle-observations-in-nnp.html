Interesting. Thank you Brian. I always thought of Martial Eagle as a bird of open and lightly wooded country. 

It seems the African Hawk-Eagles might be seriously considering nesting in NNP, if they aren't already. It's also possible that they could nest immediately south of the park in the Kitengela area as there are a couple of well wooded river valleys (along seasonal tributaries of the Mbagathi). There is definitely no shortage of game birds and small mammals for them to hunt. A recent encouraging development is that the Masai community in this area is in the process of forming a community conservancy, which would serve as a buffer against the growing Kitengela town.

Happy birding,

On Wed, Jul 13, 2016 at 12:49 PM, Brian Finch <birdfinch@gmail.com> wrote:
Dear Sidney,
Some nice records for the Park area. There was a pair of adult African
Hawk-Eagles that took up a short residence along the Mbagathi not far
from Hippo Pools Oct/Nov last year. It was hoped that they would nest,
but there was never any evidence. I would imagine this could be the
same pair that you saw.
Bateleurs have long had a nest site along the Kisembe River in the
Kisembe Forest. We cannot get there at present because of the road
becoming treacherous since the flooding. These are the only pair in
the Park but they are often along Mbagathi Gorge.
There has long been a pair of Martial Eagles also in the Kisembe
Forest, which is probably responsible for your Galleria sighting,
there can’t be many shopping malls with Martial Eagle on its list! We
frequently see them over the garden here, and they are often in the
afternoons over the Elephant Orphanage area and are undoubtedly the
same pair as Langata Gate and your Galleria bird.
Martial Eagles are not just in Kisembe Forest, but also in forests
such as Aberdares and Mt Kenya. They are in thick forest also such as
Bwindi in Uganda and Nyungwe in Rwanda, and seem quite versatile.
Best for now
Brian
