It's fascinating that the extermination of sparrows in China in 1958 to protect rice crops led to an explosion of locusts which the sparrows also ate. This in turn led to even greater loss of crop and it exacerbated the great famine in China that led to the death of up to 45 million people due to starvation.

This is a lesson we can ill afford to repeat the mistakes of China. I wonder if we have any ornithologist experts on quelea who might tell us what the likely ecological consequences are of eliminating quelea
