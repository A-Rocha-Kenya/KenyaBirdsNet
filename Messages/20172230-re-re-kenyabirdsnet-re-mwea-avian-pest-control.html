Hi,

I recall, many years ago in Botswana, seeing kites plowing through dense, airborne, flocks of Red-billed Quelea...injuring several quelea at a go and flying to the ground to kill and eat them.  In the meantime, the undamaged quelea continued to ('happily') fly-about and feed.  The 'strategy' of Red-billed Quelea seems to be to occur in such great numbers as to to 'swamp-the-predator' to the point that predation is, on an individual basis, not a big concern.

I agree, that trained falcons are unlikely to have the desired impact on quelea and other birds feeding on rice at Mwea.

Best,  Tom
