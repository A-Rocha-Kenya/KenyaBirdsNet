Subject: MANGROVE KINGFISHER, RANGE, SEASONALITY, BREEDING AND DIET
Dear All,
I have been back through the old kenyabirdsnet sightings, and was
unable to locate any records of Mangrove Kingfishers outside of the
April-November window. I was particularly  interested in the Tana
Delta as it had been suggested that it might be resident there. I did
find a very interesting piece of evidence that highly suggests that it
is a migrant there like everywhere else in coastal Kenya.
It was in the Dowsett-Lemaire's report in Oct-Dec 2006, they surveyed
the avifauna of Tana Delta based at Kipini; under Mangrove Kingfisher
they state " Very common (and noisy) in coastal thickets in Oct.,
singing in duets at dawn and dusk. Heard two singing to each other in
forest clumps opposite base camp (Oct.,early Nov.); seen at new camp.
Heard at forest edges in Witu Extension and Luimishi. Not singing in
second half of Nov. nor Dec. and then easily overlooked (none seen in
Dec.).
