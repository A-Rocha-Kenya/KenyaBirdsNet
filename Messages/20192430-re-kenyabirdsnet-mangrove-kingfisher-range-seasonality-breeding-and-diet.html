That is such a good amount of very interesting data. That 50 at Lunga
Lunga is really telling that it a major migrant, we'll have to see if
Mustafa Adamjee can look around the mangroves there in end of April as
he lives there, or at least see if numbers increase. I can't imagine
seeing a flock of Mangrove Kingfishers! It's all falling into that
April-November bracket. Pemba is likely an established resident
population which does not seem to be the case on Zanzibar. If they are
at Dar all year it could be the farthest North that the species
regularly breeds, we really need to see Neil's TZ Atlas data, because
in Tanzania it may be a breeding bird along the entire southern coast,
and possibly we'll find that north of Dar all records fall in
April-November just as with us.
