Dear all!

While preparing for the waterbird counts at Lakes Naivasha and Elmenteita last weekend, on Friday afternoon, we found a Woodchat Shrike on the lakeshore near KWS Hippo Camp (annex to KWSTI). It was still present in the same area on Sunday.

The camp is signposted off the old Naivasha road, near town; a track leads to the lakeshore but you'll need to pay KSh 100 for entering at the KWS buildings. Walk to your right from the track end, where the bird was perching on thistles at 2-300 m from the track. This is a great birding area in general (Black Herons, Water Thickknees were present as well; the counts produced a Black-headed Gull at Crescent Island).

Good birding!

Bernd de Bruijn
