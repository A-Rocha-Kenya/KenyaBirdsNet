Hi all birders
I just want to let you know that I have been out birding with mr. Wiel poelmans from Holland plus kamande a guide at museum. We went looking for the Hegulins Bustard and we managed to get about 6 of them on your way to Chalib desert about 8km from the marsabit town and again we drove about 40km north of Marsabit towards Moyale at Sigalugalu desert to look for the creseted Lark as well Thelak Lark which was productive and in plenty at about 18km from marsabit town they seemed to be alot on your way should you drive at snail speed.

Early on we had spent a day at Nairobi National Park and we saw The Short Toed lark as well leaving here we headed to wanjee camp and it was again nice to see the Hinde's babbler carrying some food to undislosed site although the guide at the camp david told us he has been seeing it for the last one week at a designated farm carrying food to the nest which means they are breeding and actually understood that they breed during the cold season and this seems to be one of their great threat to survival.

We later spent 2 days at samburu before heading to marsabit and here it was interesting to record the 2 palm nut vultures on the river banks and then one more on the road side as we headed to marsabit after a town by the name Leisamis.

We also recorded Somalia Fiscal at Chalib Desert road.

Enjoy you good birding times all

Kind regards from
Joseph Kariuki
