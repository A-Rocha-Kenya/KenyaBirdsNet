--- James Wolstencroft <gonolek@gmail.com> wrote:


On Monday 2 January I found two Chiffchaffs in
junipers adjacent to the
lowest forest edge at ca 2300m on the south side of
Mt. Meru.

Yesterday (4th) there were at least six birds in
another area some three km
from the first and a couple of hundred metres
higher. All those seen clearly
appeared to be Phylloscopus collybita abietinus and
most were in repeated
song for short periods as they foraged.

All were feeding chiefly in the canopy and sometimes
in the mid strata of
those well-established juniper plantations
(Juniperus procera ?)that have a
dense herbaceous ground flora. A moderately
bird-rich 'artificial' habitat
here on Meru which , as one would expect nowadays,
is being felled and
converted to potatoes and maize at a very much
faster rate than it is being
(re) planted.



James
