Very interesting. I've just been out at Whale island witih David Monticelli who's working 
on Roseates on the Seychelles and we found only 19 nests all of them seemingly 
abandoned though there were 15 adults flying around behaving as if they had nests and 
even two carrying food. The nests all had single eggs except one clutch of 2. One Sooty 
Tern nest as well but no adults seen (though 6 seen from the shore on Friday through a 
'scope). 

This time last year there were 800-1000 nests on Whale Island so it confirms that in 
some years they don't really breed here. Strange thing is at the end of June when I 
checked there were about 150-200 birds flying over the island but the conditions / tides 
have been wrong until now to get over there. David might try and get to Lamu to check 
on the Tenewe Island colony if we can get him permission to get onto it.

Aride have had the lowest no. of breeding pairs this year in 5 years which is also 
interesting...

Colin
