Dear All,
 
Just completed another thoroughly enjoyable trip to Kenya, despite all the troubles. A few records of more unusual birds to submit -
 
Common Redshank - 7-9 January 2008 on western shore of Lake Ololeiden (spelling? the small one anyway), Naivasha - single bird
 
Lesser Crested Tern - 9 January on eastern shore of Lake Elementaita, single bird resting on shore and flying up and down the lake edge
 
Great Snipe - 14 January, on Rift Valley floor below escarpment some 25 km south of Corner Baridi, Ngong Hills, single bird flushed from marshy patch in otherwise parched bush
 
Many thanks too to Kenyabirdsnet and Chege for the alert on the Common Cranes! 
 
And a very late record from a previous visit-
 
Ferruginous Duck - 20 November 2005, single male, also on Lake Ololeiden, Naivasha (plus at least 50 Maccao Duck)
 
Best of luck to one and all,    Jeffrey James
