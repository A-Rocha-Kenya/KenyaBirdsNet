Hi James et al.
 
Researchers have put up either Sisal, but I think bamboo 'boxes' around Mpala Research Centre.  They cut a small entrance hole and blocked off the top and bottom, not sure with what.  The biggest problem with nest boxes for any size species is bees, which are difficult to deter.  What has happened at Mpala is then the honey hunters come around and destroy the boxes.  There's been a number of attempts at Mpala.  The first attempt using bamboo boxes was largely unsuccessful, I don't recall anything nesting in them, although an occassional woodpecker could be seen around the entrance hole. The most recent attempts have been to attract starlings, but what I've heard from the team is that the majority of boxes were destroyed by honey hunters. 
 
Some colleagues in Cameroon put up nest boxes for barn owls which were immediately occupied by bees. 
 
It may be worth a try, but don't have high expectations.
Darcy
