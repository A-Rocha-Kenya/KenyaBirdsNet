On the first evening walking on  the beach there were a good number of lesser crested terns together with some smaller terns.   Also with the groups
were sooty gulls, a few crab plovers, and many ringed plovers.
 
The second day on the same beach, the majority of the lesser crested had gone, leaving only one or two but there was a large number of small grey
terns, a few of which appeared to have red bills and legs, but it was difficult to be sure.   Would Common Terns be expected?
There were many curlew sandpipers, ring necked plovers, a good scattering of sanderlings, a few ruddy turnstone, crab plovers, grey plovers and the usual sooty gulls.
 
In comparison Bofa beach had very few birds, mainly ring necked plovers, grey plovers and a few gulls.   This isnow a much more frequented beach with a marked reduction in the little ghost crabs etc.
