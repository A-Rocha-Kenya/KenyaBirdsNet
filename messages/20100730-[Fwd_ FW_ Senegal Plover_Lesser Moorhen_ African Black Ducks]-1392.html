Dear birder and all,

Am out on birding safari since 15th Aug and hoping to finish this tour by Aug 5th. Am just wishing to highlight the sighting of Senegal Plover in Samburu around choka gate first time to see them there on 19th July 2010. It was no doubt as we had photos of this as well next 2 days we were back on the road towards Nakuru and we did get the Blackwing Plover up on Timau area on wheat farms also along Solio Ranch again. My question to all of you is, has anyone ever sighted this or this could be the first time report of this species here? Kindly vanish more if you can, To Brian my respected report what are your opinion on this?

On 22nd July 2010 we spoted  5 Lesser Moorehen at Muya Causeway and my clients who are seasoned birder and professor in the UK had seen Brian report on Kenya Birdsnet and had highlighted the same. Kindly if any one of you is dying to see this species, then time is now at Lake Nakuru National Park but kindly note you are only going to see this species at it juvenile stage but there is one which has every other clear distinctions help you iron out them. On the same park we had good view of 8 African Back Ducks on the Njoro river as it drains water to the lake from the carvat bridge we had 3 together and the close  to Marabou stork roosting area there were 5 more together. Has anyone else seen this ducks at this Park?

I may report any other unusual sighting should it happen.

Sorry that am not good trip report writer as all others have been doing but thought I should highlight this to birders to keep on look.
