Dear all - it has been wonderful to see the incredible response to the news of the 50+ year old Lesser Flamingo and I think the news has almost gone viral across the planet! Ken, of BirdLife Africa, is putting something up on the BirdLife website about it and quite astutely noted that I had written "L. Baringo" when in fact there are very few (never??) flamingos on Baringo and instead they are on Bogoria. He's correct - it was a simple typo and the correct location was in fact Lake Bogoria - a distance of 242kms from where it was ringed in Lake Magadi.

Other news from here in Watamu - Carmine Bee-eaters moving over in small groups almost daily;

Sabaki waterbird count at the w/e had a few Curlew Sand and Lesser Sand  (c. 150 + 80) but otherwise very quiet. One Caspian Tern is out of season, a handful of non-breeding Common Terns and Saunders' Terns; the seasonal fresh-water pools are still wet between the dunes and the sea and we had four Greater Painted Snipe which are not common for us as well as a dozen or so Senegal Plovers.

Great weather here in Watamu right now - cool and fresh but not cold.
