Dear Birders,
On Saturday I spent whole day in Nairobi National Park. The day started with with a Grosbeak at the entrance of the Safari Walk. Near the entrance was a brown Parisoma and an Olive Thrush on a hidden water trough. Quite a number of Willow warblers on the shading trees in the parking. This is a day I have recoded such unique and many species. At the Kingfisher dam were four Pied Kingfishers and a Malachite Kingfisher, a whinchat, two Moorhens, a Purple swamphen, little grebe, Egyptian Goose, Secret Ibis, two flocks of wood Sandpipers( one flock with 38 birds and the other with 20 individuals), quite interesting. One African marsh Harrier, African reed warbler a hippo and a young crocodile. Please see the attached list of other observations.

Thanks,
