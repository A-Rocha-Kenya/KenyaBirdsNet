Those of you who were shocked by the images of Amur Falcons being harvested in Nagaland last year may be interested in this report.

http://tinyurl.com/obpyc4n

It also contains a weblink to enable you to view the satellite tracks of three Amur Falcons that are heading towards Africa.  Two left India earlier today and are now flying above the Indian Ocean.

Instructions to view the sat-track maps is below:

1.  Click on www.satellitetracking.eu.
2.  Click on 'English' along the top menu bar to get the correct language.
3.  Click on 'Birds' on the top menu bar.
4.  Search down page 1 of the results and find - Naga, Pangti and Wokha.
5.  Click the little white boxes to the left of all three names.
6.  Go to the bottom of the page and click on Send Map.

You can then zoom in/out using the scale in the top left.

And reposition the map by clicking, holding down and dragging it across your screen.

Enjoy!
