I actually put the yellow-billed duck picture out as a teaser to see what response would arise. While I agree the green / blue of the speculum is a structural colour depending on the angle of the light etc, in this case both ducks are in similar pose and angle, but the colour difference is striking, and was so through the binoculars also.

 

Zimmerman, Turner and Pearson note that the nominate Anas undulata undulata has a green speculum, while “old records from Marsabit may refer to A. u. rueppelli, as may recent sightings of blue-speculumed birds at Ol Bolossat.”

 

Mackworth-Praed and Grant (now that’s going back a bit!) give A.u. rueppelli as differing from the nominate race in having the speculum more blue than green, with a range in Ethiopia, northern Kenya and northern Uganda.

 

I believe we have here a rueppelli wanderer from the north hanging out with some local undulata.
