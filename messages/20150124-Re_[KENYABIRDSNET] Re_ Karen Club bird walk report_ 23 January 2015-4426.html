Hi Patrick & Mike, 

Thanks for those comments. Patrick, I agree that the Brown-backed Woodpecker is fairly common throughout the forested areas of Karen, and elsewhere in Nairobi.

To expand on my comments about it being more common around Nairobi than is supposed, I think it is one of a group of species that can be underreported because it has some resemblance to a similar species that is more common - in this case, the Cardinal Woodpecker - and it takes a certain level of awareness by even experienced birders to sort them out. 

Others in this category could include Thick-billed Seedeater (Streaky Seedeater), Red-faced Cisticola (Singing Cisticola), Scarce Swift (Palm Swift), Collared Sunbird (Variable Sunbird), and Slender-billed Greenbul (various canopy warblers). 

An interesting point for discussion!!

All best, JD
