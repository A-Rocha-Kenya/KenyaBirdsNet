Many thanks for the interesting observations - pleased that there are still some field naturalists that use binoculars to study animals (nothing against DNA, but it is limited).  If the observers take a look at my monograph on sengis (elephant-shrews) - "The Social Structure and Ecology of Elephant-shrews", Paul Parey, Adv. Ethol. 20 on page 54, they will find a few data on exactly what they observed (although not with the thrush, which is not at all surprising to me).  Attached a little piece with an illustration (sorry about the big file size).  Of course more extensive quantification would be nice, but few people now days spend the focused time in the field to take it the next step!  A copy of the monograph can be downloaded from the literature cited section at this site: http://www.sengis.org/biological-synopsis.php.  In any case, perhaps you could pass this on to the keen observers.  Thanks.
