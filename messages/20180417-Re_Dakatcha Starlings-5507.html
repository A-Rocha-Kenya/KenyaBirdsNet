Dear Don, James, Washington and all

Thank you for your comments regarding the starlings with reddish-brown
immatures that we saw in Dakatcha Woodland in late March.

At the time, I must confess that we were just confused. I was already
confused as to whether the Greater Blue-eared Starlings in Dakatcha were the
coastal and inland race. The focus of our monitoring was on forest birds, so
the starlings in the trees above abandoned farmland were not high priority.
I was not even aware of Southern Blue-eared Starlings.

The starlings were too far to be photographed with the little camera we had.
But I saw them well with binoculars, and some ­ evidently the immatures ­
seemed to be entirely rufous-brown below. Then I remembered that immatures
of many glossy starlings are brownish.

Blue-eared starlings in Dakatcha obviously needs more observation. Would
anyone like to organize a visit to Dakatcha Woodland?

Good birding to all, Fleur
