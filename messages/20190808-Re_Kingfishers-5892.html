Difficult to say Fleur.  First the Pygmy Kingfisher:  the southern migrant (April-September) does reach central Kenya and is best distinguished from the nominate form by its very pale almost whitish belly (northern birds are all pale rufous-orange below). Regarding the Grey-headed, being as you have been seeing it on a regular basis its probably the nominate form (silvery grey head and tawny belly). Southern migrants are mainly western (Lake Victoria basin) and have pale orange-buff bellies.  Northern migrants are largely nominate birds, but the very richly coloured (brown capped) form from Yemen does get to Kenya on occasions as Brian found recently.

While easy to identify to species, kingfisher subspecies are much more difficult and require good looks at them, even better with photographs.

Hope this helps.
Don
